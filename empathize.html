MAPeco - Empathize

Having studied Qualitative Research Methods, we chose interviews to discover what do people think regarding the topic of plastic bags. However, we did not want to suggest anything, so we built a questionnaire with questions about the waste in general with some questions connected to plastic bags.

Each one of us interviewed 2 people (separately), so there were 8 people interviewed in total. Interviewees were adults and constituted a diverse group of people: including heads of families, people living with friends or living alone, residents of villages, small or large cities.

The most interesting about these interviews was the fact that people put more focus on sorting out rubbish and reducing waste rather than the plastic bags themselves. So we adapted to their pains. We discoreved that some of the main reasons that discourage people are: being forced to use plastic, not enough space for separate recycling bins in their houses, no unified segregation system or lack of initiative and action on the part of others. What motivated our interviewees were: being proud of doing something good for the environment or taking care of the health of loved ones. They also shared what could encourage them to do something in this topic: feeling that it makes sense, some pattern on how to deal with waste or saving money.
